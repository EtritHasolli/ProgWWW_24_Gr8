Study Method 1

Cornell Notes: A note-taking system that involves dividing your notes into cues, notes, and summary sections.

Study Method 2

Focused Notes: A method where you take highly focused notes on key concepts to reinforce understanding.

Study Method 3

Flash Cards: A method of reviewing information by writing questions on one side and answers on the other.

Study Method 4

Blurting: Writing everything you know about a topic to identify knowledge gaps and improve recall.

Study Method 5

Spaced Repetition: Reviewing material at increasing intervals to boost long-term memory retention.

Study Method 6

Feynman Technique: Simplifying complex concepts by explaining them in simple language.

Study Method 7

Power Nap: A short 10-20 minute nap that boosts energy and cognitive function.

Study Method 8

Second Brain: Organizing information externally to free up mental space and improve retention.

Study Method 9

Time Blocking: Dividing your day into blocks of focused study time and rest.

Study Method 10

Socratic Method: Asking and answering questions to stimulate critical thinking and understanding.

Study Method 11

Active Recall: Testing your knowledge by recalling information without looking at your notes.

Study Method 12

SQ3R Method: A reading comprehension method involving Survey, Question, Read, Recite, Review.
